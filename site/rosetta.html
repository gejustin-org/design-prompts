Handshake Design System

Rosetta

Professional clarity with human warmth. A design language for platforms connecting students and employers. Readable, accessible, trustworthy.

Philosophy

The Visual Vibe

Clean. Approachable. Trustworthy. Modern.

Imagine a well-designed career center: organized, professional, but not intimidating. The interface feels helpful rather than bureaucratic. It's the difference between a sterile government form and a thoughtfully designed onboarding experience.

If this design were a physical space:

A modern coworking space with clean desks and good lighting. White walls with occasional accent plants. Natural light through large windows. Professional but not stuffy - people wear nice casual. Quiet enough to focus, social enough to feel human.

Emotional Keywords

  • Trustworthy - Students trust this platform with their career. The design must feel reliable, not experimental.
  • Approachable - Despite handling serious workflows (hiring, applications), it never feels cold or corporate.
  • Clear - Information hierarchy is paramount. Users scan, not read. Every visual decision aids comprehension.
  • Modern - Contemporary without being trendy. Noi Grotesk brings geometric precision with humanist warmth.
  • Neutral - The content (jobs, profiles, companies) is the hero. The UI recedes.
Colors

Typography

Noi Grotesk with Humanist Warmth

A geometric sans-serif with optical corrections and humanist touches. The ss03 and ss11 stylistic alternates give it distinctive character without sacrificing readability. Using Inter as a web fallback.

Signature

The Bold Factor

What makes Rosetta distinctive:

Dark Teal, Not Blue

#04191B is the hero color. Sophisticated, distinctive, avoids "generic SaaS blue."

Noi Grotesk + Stylistic Alternates

ss03 and ss11 features give typography subtle character without sacrificing legibility.

Shadow-First Elevation

Cards rely on soft shadows rather than heavy borders. Depth without visual noise.

4px Grid Discipline

Everything snaps to the 4px grid. Consistency creates subconscious harmony.

Dual-Variant Status Colors

Each status has both a light background tint and saturated foreground for flexible patterns.

White Space as Feature

Generous padding and margins. The interface breathes. Content has room.

Semantic Color Restraint

Color means something. Blue = info. Green = success. Red = error. Never decorative.

Sansplomb for Impact

Bold condensed face reserved for marketing/brand moments when you need to make a statement.
