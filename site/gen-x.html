Gen X_

Born 1965 - 1980

MTV era grunge meets early internet. VHS tracking lines, CRT phosphor glow, and DIY independence. The visual language of zines, mixtapes, dial-up modems, and the belief that trying too hard is the ultimate sin.

Design Philosophy

Deliberately imperfect, anti-corporate, analog-meets-digital. Nothing is polished. Edges are rough. Type is distressed. The aesthetic actively rejects the slick professionalism of the 80s yuppie era in favor of authenticity.

Core Pillars

  • The Zine Aesthetic: Photocopied textures, cut-and-paste collage, ransom-note typography
  • VHS/CRT Artifacts: Tracking lines, static noise, RGB bleeding, phosphor glow
  • Grunge Typography: Distressed fonts, typewriter keys, broken rules as features
  • "Under Construction" Web 1.0: Animated GIFs, tiled backgrounds, visitor counters
  • DIY Ethos: Everything looks handmade, even when it's not
